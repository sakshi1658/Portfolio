Project Overview

NAMAN is a government-funded mental health initiative under the Tele-MANAS program, led by NIMHANS and AHT, to improve mental health access in underserved areas. The web application is used by Community Health Workers (CHWs), Primary Health Centers (PHCs), and district-level psychiatrists for mental health screenings, tracking, and referrals.

My Contributions

  • Developed responsive mobile & tablet views of the platform using Angular and Bootstrap, ensuring seamless usability across devices.
  • Integrated dynamic screening and consent forms with backend REST APIs to store and retrieve CHW-collected data.
  • Collaborated with senior developers to fix critical UI bugs and enhance overall performance and UX of the portal.
  • Wrote multiple unit tests and participated in end-to-end testing for various functionalities.
  • Documented functional workflows and participated in daily stand-ups and client calls to ensure alignment and efficient issue resolution.

Key Features of the Platform

  • Role-based access: CHW, PHC, MO, and psychiatrist modules
  • Consent Management & Digital Signature support
  • MERIT screening tool integration for mental health evaluation
  • Dynamic appointment scheduling across facilities
  • Offline-first design considerations for remote areas

Impact

This platform is actively being used by CHWs in Karnataka and Uttarakhand to screen and refer patients under the national Tele-MANAS program. It reduces delays in care, improves access to mental health professionals, and supports India’s mission of inclusive digital healthcare.
